Academic profile

AI for Scientific Data Generation and Space Weather Prediction

I am MohammadReza EskandariNasab, also known as Sam, a name associated with a mythical hero of ancient Persia and an important figure in the Shahnameh. I am pursuing a PhD in Computer Science at Utah State University, where I also earned my MSc in Computer Science.

My research focuses on generative modeling, particularly for time-series data. I have developed models based on diffusion methods, generative adversarial networks, and adversarial autoencoders, with publications in venues including IEEE ICDM, SIAM SDM, IEEE BigData, and IEEE ICMLA. My recent work also explores multimodal learning through an NSF-funded project (Award No. 2530946) on diffusion-based generation and translation across images, text, vector data, and time-series data.

I also work on space-weather prediction, developing data-processing pipelines and machine-learning classifiers for forecasting solar flares, solar energetic particle events, and coronal mass ejections. My research has appeared in The Astrophysical Journal Supplement Series, with additional manuscripts under review at The Astrophysical Journal Supplement Series and The Astrophysical Journal. I have also contributed to multiple NSF-funded projects, including Award No. 2301397, addressing key challenges in space-weather forecasting.

In addition, I design and develop cyberinfrastructure for scientific data analysis and space-weather applications. One example is FlaPLeT.org, an NSF-funded platform (Award No. 2305781) that integrates backend and frontend systems within a graphical, no-code environment for data preprocessing, classifier training, and model evaluation. FlaPLeT enables users to upload datasets, prepare data, train machine-learning models, and assess performance without writing code. The platform was published in SoftwareX.
